Activation Visualizer

An interactive tool to inspect how activations behave across layers for common nonlinearities (e.g., ReLU, GELU, SiLU). It helps you spot dead neurons, saturation, and vanishing/exploding trends before or during training.

  • Layer-by-layer distributions: histograms and summary stats (mean/variance) of activations.
  • Saturation & dead-ReLU rate: quick indicators when units clamp to 0 or extremes.
  • Pre/post nonlinearity view: compare signals right before vs. after the activation.
  • Depth diagnostics: track how activation scale drifts as you go deeper.
  • Tunable inputs: experiment with input variance/scale to see stability regions.

Polar-Express / Muon Orthogonalization Visualizer

Visualizes orthogonalization methods used in optimizers like Muon. Iterative schemes such as Polar Express drive singular values toward 1 using only matrix–matrix multiplies, avoiding costly SVD while improving conditioning of weight updates.

Reference iterations often compared:

  • Polar Express: accelerated polynomial scaling with cushioning/safety.
  • Newton–Schulz: locally convergent quadratic iteration: Xk+1 = 0.5 · Xk(3I − XkTXk).
  • Matrix Sign (Jordan): robust but inversion-heavy: Xk+1 = 0.5 · (Xk + Xk−T).
